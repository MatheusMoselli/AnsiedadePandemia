Technology addiction & Mental Health.

As the virus spread through the world, our society had to isolate itself, becoming dependent on technology not just for entertainment but also for work and school. It became such a primary part of our lives that even now, when the storm is already gone, we can't think about living without the internet. Now, think about it: did the pandemic helped us progress as a society, or did it just brought more problems?
Let's find out.

Positive side.

Even before the pandemic, we were already used to technology; most people don't leave home without their cellphone, and a lot of companies need computers to keep their business. As I said, it's not something new, though our relationship with it turned from "a helpful device" to a life necessity. Technology is the reason why society kept working in these tough times, and we are the reason why it developed so much. Digital payments, remote working/learning, stronger web security systems, more accurate AIs, and even medical appointments from home. These aren't temporary changes, most of them, if not all, will carry on in our lives, and there's no changing it. Though they add a lot to our society, you know that no good doesn't come with a price.

Negative side.

Not being able to work presently or even get out of home was a big shock to everyone. Your work, your school, your whole life was trapped inside your house; so, eventually, the consequences arrived. Loneliness, burnout, procrastination, depression, anxiety, and I'm not even considering all the economic problems that were caused. Internet was the way that society found to keep working, though some people took advantage of it. For example, the cost of cybercrimes will rise to about 6 trillion dollars a year by the end of 2021. We also had several cases of cyber-attacks and online frauds, and also the fake news grew a lot, mainly because of the vaccines and elections in the US.

Conclusion

Technology is a part of our lives now, and there's no way out of it. It brought a lot of positive effects to our society, but the problems can be just as present. Nonetheless, we'll always find a way to improve and get stronger as a collective, just as we did in the past.

Mental health NGOs

Mental health NGOs are humanitarian organizations that are committed to provide psychological assistance to people who suffers from socio-affective-psychological vulnerability.

Instituto Sem Grilo

O ISG viabiliza acesso à saúde mental a pessoas assistidas por instituições sociais reconhecidas ao criar uma conexão entre profissionais voluntários e instituições apoiadas.

Centro de valorização da vida

O CVV — Centro de Valorização da Vida, fundado em São Paulo, em 1962, é uma associação civil sem fins lucrativos, filantrópica, reconhecida como de Utilidade Pública Federal, desde 1973. Presta serviço voluntário e gratuito de apoio emocional e prevenção do suicídio para todas as pessoas que querem e precisam conversar, sob total sigilo e anonimato.
