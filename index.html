Technology addiction & Mental Health.

As the virus spread through the world, our society had to isolate itself, becoming dependent on technology not just for entertainment but also for work and school. It became such a primary part of our lives that even now, when the storm is already gone, we can't think about living without the internet. Now, think about it: did the pandemic helped us progress as a society, or did it just bring more problems?
Let's find out.

Positive side.

Even before the pandemic, we were already used to technology; most people don't leave home without their cellphone, and a lot of companies need computers to keep their business. As I said, it's not something new, though our relationship with it turned from "a helpful device" to a life necessity. Technology is the reason why society kept working in these tough times, and our condition is the reason why it developed so much. Digital payments, remote working/learning, stronger web security systems, more accurate AIs, and even medical appointments from home. These aren't temporary changes, most of them, if not all, will carry on in our lives, and there's no changing it. Though they add a lot to our society, you know that no good doesn't come with a price.

Negative side.

Not being able to work presently or even get out of home was a big shock to everyone. Your work, your school, your whole life was trapped inside your house; so, eventually, the consequences arrived. Loneliness, burnout, procrastination, depression, anxiety, and I'm not even considering all the economic problems that were caused. Internet was the way that society found to keep working, though some people took advantage of it. For example, the cost of cybercrimes will rise to about 6 trillion dollars a year by the end of 2021. We also had several cases of cyber-attacks and online frauds, and also the fake news grew a lot, mainly because of the vaccines and elections in the US.

Anxiety

Isolation isn't something easy to deal with; the struggles of being alone most of the time can drive people to irrational states of mind. Humans aren't built for loneliness, so transitioning to this lifestyle in such a sudden way was a struggle for most of us. Social media has become almost a stress releasing mechanism in these dark times, though it might just bring even more mental health problems. Engagement is now portrayed as how much people like you; cyberbullying has grown so much, but now coming in different shapes or forms; miscommunication has never been so predominant. The reality from our screen is completely distorted from the truth, but it's either this or being alone with your own thoughts. It's not a fair choice, although nowadays it's easier to seek help for mental health issues. Disconnecting for a while isn't a bad idea. Sometimes, we need to remind ourselves that we are only in control of our actions, and even that the whole world seems a completely messy, everything's okay...

80% of Brazilians suffer from anxiety

Conclusion

Technology is a part of our lives now, and there's no way out of it. It brought a lot of positive effects to our society, but the problems can be just as present. Nonetheless, we'll always find a way to improve and get stronger as a collective, just as we did in the past.

Mental health NGOs

Mental health NGOs are humanitarian organizations that are committed to provide psychological assistance to people who suffers from socio-affective-psychological vulnerability.

Instituto Sem Grilo

The ISG provides access to mental support for people who is assisted by social institutions by creating a connection between volunteer professionals and supported institutions.

Centro de valorização da vida

CVV — Center for the Valorization of Life, was founded in São Paulo, on 1962. This NGO is a non-profit and philanthropic civil association recognized as a Federal Public Utility since 1973. It provides voluntary and free emotional support and suicide prevention services for all the people who want and need to talk, in total secrecy and anonymity.

Instituto bem do estar

We, from the Instituto Bem do Estar, believe that the first step to minimize the stigma that involves mental health, mental disorders and suicide is to TALK ABOUT THE MATTER, learn more about the mental health, prevent mental suffering and its consequences.
